# Jean Fouquet

## Basic Information
- **Full Name:** Jean Fouquet (or Jehan Fouquet)
- **Born:** c. 1420, Tours, France
- **Died:** c. 1481, Tours, France
- **Nationality:** French
- **Period:** Late Middle Ages / Early French Renaissance

## Artistic Style
Jean Fouquet was the most important French painter of the 15th century. He combined the tradition of International Gothic with innovations from the Italian Renaissance, creating a distinctive French style.

### Key Characteristics:
- Synthesis of French Gothic and Italian Renaissance styles
- Master of manuscript illumination
- Precise architectural settings
- Clear, balanced compositions
- Subtle use of perspective
- Cool, refined color palette
- Integration of contemporary French life into religious scenes

## Notable Works

### Major Commissions:
1. **Melun Diptych** (c. 1450-1455)
   - Originally in Melun Cathedral
   - Left panel: Étienne Chevalier with St. Stephen (Gemäldegalerie, Berlin)
   - Right panel: Virgin and Child (Royal Museum of Fine Arts, Antwerp)
   - Famous for the pale, sculptural depiction of the Virgin

2. **Hours of Étienne Chevalier** (c. 1452-1460)
   - Illuminated manuscript
   - Miniatures now scattered in various collections (Musée Condé, Chantilly)
   - Masterpiece of manuscript illumination
   - 47 surviving miniatures from original 60

3. **Antiquités Judaïques** (c. 1470-1476)
   - Illuminated manuscript of Josephus's Jewish Antiquities
   - Location: Bibliothèque nationale de France, Paris
   - Contains detailed historical scenes

4. **Self-Portrait** (c. 1450)
   - Location: Louvre Museum, Paris
   - Enamel medallion
   - One of the earliest independent self-portraits in European art

5. **Book of Hours of Simon de Varie** (c. 1455)
   - Illuminated prayer book
   - Shows his mature illumination style

## Historical Significance

Fouquet's importance includes:
- Bringing Italian Renaissance innovations to France
- Establishing the French portrait tradition
- Elevating manuscript illumination to high art
- Combining religious art with contemporary French settings

## Career and Patronage

Fouquet served:
- The royal court of Charles VII
- Étienne Chevalier, treasurer to Charles VII (major patron)
- Louis XI, who appointed him royal painter

## Italian Influence

Around 1445-1447, Fouquet traveled to Rome where he:
- Painted a portrait of Pope Eugene IV (now lost)
- Studied Italian Renaissance art
- Absorbed perspective and compositional techniques
- Met contemporary Italian masters

This Italian journey profoundly influenced his style, which he then adapted to French tastes.

## Manuscript Illumination

Fouquet revolutionized manuscript illumination by:
- Treating miniatures as panel paintings
- Using Renaissance perspective in small format
- Including contemporary French costumes and architecture
- Creating narrative clarity and spatial depth

## Legacy

Fouquet established:
- The tradition of French Renaissance painting
- High standards for court portraiture
- Integration of realism with decorative elegance
- The French manuscript illumination tradition

His work influenced French art for generations and represents the pinnacle of medieval manuscript illumination.

## Contemporary Recognition

As court painter to three successive French kings and the treasurer, Fouquet enjoyed high status and numerous commissions. He was recognized as France's greatest painter during his lifetime.
