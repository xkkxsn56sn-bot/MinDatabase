# Duccio di Buoninsegna

## Basic Information
- **Full Name:** Duccio di Buoninsegna
- **Born:** c. 1255-1260, Siena, Italy
- **Died:** c. 1318-1319, Siena, Italy
- **Nationality:** Italian (Sienese)
- **Period:** Late Middle Ages / Italian Gothic

## Artistic Style
Duccio was the founder of the Sienese school of painting. While contemporary with Giotto, he maintained closer ties to Byzantine traditions while infusing them with Gothic elegance and emotional depth.

### Key Characteristics:
- Byzantine influences with Gothic refinement
- Rich gold backgrounds
- Delicate, graceful figures
- Sophisticated color palette
- Attention to decorative detail
- Emotional expressiveness in faces

## Notable Works

### Major Commissions:
1. **Maestà** (1308-1311)
   - Location: Originally Siena Cathedral, now Museo dell'Opera del Duomo
   - Massive altarpiece showing the Virgin Mary in majesty
   - Double-sided with multiple panels
   - Considered his masterpiece
   - Celebrated with a grand procession when installed

2. **Rucellai Madonna** (c. 1285)
   - Location: Uffizi Gallery, Florence
   - Large panel painting of the Virgin and Child
   - One of his earliest major works

3. **Madonna and Child with Saints** (c. 1300)
   - Various smaller panel paintings
   - Characteristic of his refined style

## Historical Significance

Duccio's contributions to art history include:
- Establishing Siena as a major art center rivaling Florence
- Blending Byzantine tradition with Gothic innovation
- Creating emotionally engaging religious narratives
- Influencing generations of Sienese painters

## Legacy

The Sienese school, founded by Duccio, continued his artistic traditions for centuries. His students and followers, including Simone Martini and the Lorenzetti brothers, carried on his elegant, decorative style.

## Artistic Approach

Unlike Giotto's emphasis on three-dimensional space and naturalism, Duccio focused on:
- Linear grace and elegant contours
- Rich, luminous colors
- Gold-leaf backgrounds
- Spiritual and transcendent qualities
- Byzantine iconographic traditions adapted for Gothic sensibilities

## Contemporary Recognition

Duccio was highly regarded during his lifetime, receiving major commissions from the Sienese government and church. The installation of his Maestà was a city-wide celebration, demonstrating his importance to Siena.
