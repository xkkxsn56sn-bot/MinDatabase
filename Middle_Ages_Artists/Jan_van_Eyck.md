## Artistic Style
Jan van Eyck was a Flemish painter who perfected the technique of oil painting. He is considered one of the greatest painters of the Northern Renaissance and a master of naturalistic detail.

### Key Characteristics:
- Pioneering use of oil paint techniques
- Extraordinary attention to minute detail
- Realistic rendering of textures (fabrics, metals, glass)
- Complex use of light and reflection
- Hidden symbolism in everyday objects
- Vivid, luminous colors
- Precise perspective

## Notable Works

### Major Commissions:
1. **Ghent Altarpiece (Adoration of the Mystic Lamb)** (1432)
   - Location: St. Bavo's Cathedral, Ghent, Belgium
   - Completed with/after his brother Hubert van Eyck
   - Multi-panel masterpiece, one of the most influential paintings in art history
   - Incredibly detailed and complex composition

2. **Arnolfini Portrait** (1434)
   - Location: National Gallery, London
   - Double portrait with hidden symbolism
   - Famous for the convex mirror showing the artist
   - Masterpiece of Northern Renaissance art

3. **Man in a Red Turban** (1433)
   - Location: National Gallery, London
   - Possibly a self-portrait
   - Demonstrates his skill in realistic portraiture

4. **Madonna of Chancellor Rolin** (c. 1435)
   - Location: Louvre Museum, Paris
   - Shows the Virgin Mary and Christ child with Chancellor Nicolas Rolin
   - Detailed landscape visible through arches

5. **Portrait of Margaret van Eyck** (1439)
   - Location: Groeningemuseum, Bruges
   - Portrait of his wife
   - Inscribed with his motto "Als Ik Kan" (As I Can)

## Historical Significance

Jan van Eyck revolutionized painting through:
- Perfecting oil painting technique, allowing for greater detail and color depth
- Establishing new standards for realism and naturalism
- Influencing the development of portraiture
- Combining religious symbolism with everyday realism

## Technical Innovation

Van Eyck's oil painting technique included:
- Multiple thin, translucent layers (glazing)
- Binding pigments with linseed oil
- Allowing for subtle gradations and luminous effects
- Enabling unprecedented detail and precision

## Legacy

Van Eyck's innovations influenced:
- All subsequent Flemish and Dutch painters
- The development of oil painting across Europe
- Standards for realistic representation
- The Northern Renaissance art movement

## Contemporary Recognition

Jan van Eyck served as court painter to Philip the Good, Duke of Burgundy, one of the most powerful rulers in Europe. He was highly respected, trusted with diplomatic missions, and received the ducal motto "Als Ik Kan" (As I Can), which he inscribed on his works.

## Artistic Philosophy

Van Eyck's work embodied the Northern Renaissance belief that:
- The visible world reflects divine creation
- Every detail of nature deserves careful observation
- Material objects can carry spiritual meaning
- Art should combine technical mastery with symbolic depth
