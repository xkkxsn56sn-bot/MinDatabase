# Giotto di Bondone

## Basic Information
- **Full Name:** Giotto di Bondone
- **Born:** c. 1267, Vespignano (near Florence), Italy
- **Died:** January 8, 1337, Florence, Italy
- **Nationality:** Italian
- **Period:** Late Middle Ages / Proto-Renaissance

## Artistic Style
Giotto is considered the father of European painting and the first of the great Italian masters. He broke away from the Byzantine style that dominated medieval art and introduced a more naturalistic approach to painting.

### Key Characteristics:
- Three-dimensional representation of figures
- Emotional expressiveness in faces
- Use of perspective and depth
- Realistic human proportions
- Dramatic storytelling through art

## Notable Works

### Major Commissions:
1. **Scrovegni Chapel Frescoes** (1303-1305)
   - Location: Padua, Italy
   - Contains 37 scenes from the lives of Christ and the Virgin Mary
   - Considered his masterpiece

2. **Ognissanti Madonna** (c. 1310)
   - Location: Uffizi Gallery, Florence
   - Shows the Virgin Mary enthroned with angels and saints

3. **Campanile (Bell Tower)** (1334)
   - Location: Florence Cathedral
   - Served as chief architect
   - Gothic-style bell tower still standing today

4. **Basilica of Saint Francis** (c. 1290s)
   - Location: Assisi, Italy
   - Frescoes depicting the life of St. Francis

## Historical Significance

Giotto revolutionized Western art by:
- Moving away from flat, stylized Byzantine art
- Introducing naturalism and emotional depth
- Pioneering techniques that would influence the Renaissance
- Establishing a new standard for religious narrative painting

## Legacy

Giotto's innovations laid the groundwork for the Renaissance. His pupils and followers spread his techniques throughout Italy. Dante Alighieri mentioned him in the Divine Comedy, indicating his fame during his lifetime.

## Contemporary Recognition

During his life, Giotto was celebrated as the greatest painter of his time. He worked for popes, kings, and wealthy patrons across Italy, receiving numerous prestigious commissions.
