# Andrei Rublev

## Basic Information
- **Full Name:** Andrei Rublev (Андрей Рублёв)
- **Born:** c. 1360, possibly near Moscow, Russia
- **Died:** c. January 29, 1428 or 1430, Moscow, Russia
- **Nationality:** Russian
- **Period:** Late Middle Ages / Medieval Russian art

## Artistic Style
Andrei Rublev is the most celebrated medieval Russian icon painter. He created works of extraordinary spiritual depth and artistic beauty within the Byzantine icon painting tradition.

### Key Characteristics:
- Byzantine iconographic tradition
- Soft, harmonious colors
- Gentle, contemplative expressions
- Graceful, flowing lines
- Spiritual luminosity
- Perfect balance and composition
- Serene, peaceful atmosphere

## Notable Works

### Major Commissions:
1. **Trinity (The Hospitality of Abraham)** (c. 1411-1425)
   - Location: Tretyakov Gallery, Moscow
   - His masterpiece and Russia's most famous icon
   - Depicts the three angels visiting Abraham (representing the Holy Trinity)
   - Revolutionary in its composition and theology
   - Considered the perfect icon

2. **Annunciation Cathedral Frescoes** (1405)
   - Location: Moscow Kremlin
   - Worked alongside Theophanes the Greek and Prokhor of Gorodets
   - Iconostasis paintings

3. **Dormition Cathedral Frescoes** (1408)
   - Location: Vladimir, Russia
   - Collaborated with Daniel Chorny
   - Last Judgment scenes
   - Some of the finest medieval Russian frescoes

4. **Savior in Glory** (c. 1408-1410)
   - Part of the Dormition Cathedral iconostasis
   - Shows Christ enthroned

5. **Zvenigorod Icons** (early 15th century)
   - Christ the Savior, Archangel Michael, Apostle Paul
   - Discovered in 1918
   - Show his mature style

## Historical Significance

Rublev's contributions to art and spirituality:
- Created the ideal expression of Russian Orthodox spirituality
- Established standards for Russian icon painting
- Synthesized Byzantine tradition with Russian sensibility
- Influenced Russian art for centuries

## Monastic Life

Rublev was a monk at:
- Andronikov Monastery in Moscow
- Trinity-Sergius Lavra
- His religious life deeply influenced his spiritual art

## The Trinity Icon

His masterpiece, the Trinity icon:
- Commissioned to honor Sergius of Radonezh
- Revolutionary circular composition symbolizing divine unity
- Three angels seated around a table in perfect harmony
- Theology expressed through pure artistic form
- Became the model for all subsequent Trinity icons

## Canonization

Andrei Rublev was canonized by the Russian Orthodox Church in 1988, recognition of:
- His holy life as a monk
- His contribution to Orthodox spirituality
- The sacred nature of his art

## Legacy

Rublev's influence includes:
- Setting the standard for Russian icon painting
- Establishing the Moscow school of icon painting
- Inspiring Russian artists for centuries
- Representing the golden age of Russian medieval art

## The Stoglav Council (1551)

The Church Council of 1551 declared that icon painters should follow Rublev's style as the model, officially establishing him as the greatest Russian icon painter.

## Contemporary Recognition

During his lifetime, Rublev was recognized as a master and worked on the most important religious commissions in Russia. His collaboration with Theophanes the Greek, the leading icon painter of the time, indicates his high standing.

## Artistic Philosophy

Rublev's icons embody:
- Hesychast spirituality (inner stillness and prayer)
- Divine light and transfiguration
- Harmony and peace
- The invisible made visible
- Unity of form and spiritual content
