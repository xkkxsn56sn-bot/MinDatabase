# MinDatabase
A scholarly database of detailed historical essays on medieval Italian artists, focusing on Tuscan painters and sculptors from the 13th-14th centuries (Duecento and Trecento periods).

## Overview
This database documents the lives, works, and artistic networks of medieval Italian masters through comprehensive academic essays. Each entry provides detailed analysis of patronage relationships, technical innovations, archival documentation, and family workshop dynamics that shaped early Italian art.

## Stylistic Evolutions Documented

### Crucifixion Iconography
- **Christus triumphans → Christus patiens**: Giunta Pisano pioneered suffering Christ; adopted by Coppo di Marcovaldo, [Cimabue]*, Giotto
- **Technical innovations**: Green underpainting (Giunta), chiaroscuro modeling (Coppo), volumetric naturalism (Giotto)

### Spatial Representation
- **Byzantine planarity → Proto-Renaissance depth**: Maestro delle Storie di Isacco's revolutionary architectural perspective (1290s) precedes Giotto's Arena Chapel innovations

### Marian Imagery
- **Maniera Greca → Gothic refinement**: Coppo's Madonna del Bordone (1261) influenced [Duccio]* and later Sienese tradition

## Using This Database

### For Art Historical Research
Each essay provides:
- **Archival documentation**: Payment records, contracts, institutional references with exact dates
- **Patronage analysis**: Ecclesiastical, civic, and private commissioning networks
- **Technical details**: Materials (ultramarine, gold leaf), techniques (giornata fresco, tempera), workshop practices
- **Stylistic positioning**: Relationship to Byzantine tradition, Proto-Renaissance innovations, Gothic influences

### For Understanding Medieval Workshops
The database emphasizes:
- **Family transmission**: Father-son training (Coppo-Salerno, Berlinghiero-Bonaventura, Memmo-Lippo)
- **Guild structures**: Florentine painter guilds, goldsmith backgrounds (Memmo's father Filippuccio)
- **Collaborative commissions**: Multi-artist projects at Assisi, Pistoia Cathedral programs
- **Economic realities**: Payment schedules, material costs, delayed compensation, bail arrangements

### Navigation by Cross-Reference
Artists are interconnected through:
- **Direct mentions**: Follow names to related entries
- **Stylistic influence**: Trace innovations from master to pupil
- **Geographic movement**: Track artists along pilgrimage routes (Via Francigena) and trade networks
- **Patronage overlaps**: Franciscan/Dominican commissions connecting multiple masters

## Temporal Scope
**c. 1190–1400**: From Giunta Pisano's birth to end of Trecento
- Focus: Duecento-Trecento transition (Byzantine → Proto-Renaissance)
- Excludes: High Renaissance post-1400 (outside temporal scope)
